There is a particular kind of exhaustion that comes not from overwork but from overcomplexity. We have become accustomed to routines that demand a great deal from us—twelve steps in the morning, another ten at night, products applied in precise sequence under penalty of reduced efficacy.

We would like to gently push back against this.

## The Origin of Complexity

The proliferation of skincare steps did not emerge from dermatological evidence. It emerged from commerce. Each additional product is an additional purchase, an additional relationship with a brand, an additional point of dependency. The industry that told you the most important step was cleansing is the same industry that later told you cleansing required two separate products, applied in strict order, with a tonic to follow.

We are not above commerce. We sell things, and we believe in the things we sell. But we would rather sell you three products that genuinely serve your skin than ten that collectively serve our revenue.

## What Your Skin Actually Needs

A dermatologist would tell you, with some consistency, that skin requires three things: cleansing, moisture, and sun protection. Everything else is either an enhancement of these fundamentals or a corrective for a specific condition.

This does not mean serums are fraudulent. The Lumière Renewing Serum does something that moisturiser alone cannot—it delivers a concentration of fermented vitamin C to the epidermis with a precision that a cream's occlusive base cannot match. But it is an enhancement, not a necessity. If your mornings are constrained, omit the serum before omitting the SPF.

## The Ritual Case

There is another argument for simplicity, and it has nothing to do with efficiency.

A two-product morning routine can be attended to with full presence. You can feel the temperature of the water, smell the particular quality of a well-formulated cleanser, notice the way your skin responds to each application. This is not self-indulgence—it is attention, and attention has a restorative quality that rushing through twelve steps cannot provide.

We find that the people who use our products for the longest time are those who use the fewest of them. They have learned their skin. They know what it needs. And they have built a ritual they can actually sustain.

## Our Recommendation

We suggest beginning with three: a cleanser, a serum, and a moisturiser. Add a fourth—a dedicated eye treatment, perhaps, or a weekly mask—only when those three have become genuinely habitual, applied consistently for a minimum of four weeks.

Measure the results not by how much you are doing, but by how your skin looks and feels after thirty days. Trust that evidence above our instructions, above the instructions of any other brand, and above the instructions of anyone who has not met your skin.
